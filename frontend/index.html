              AquaVision

              Satellite-powered water quality monitoring for a healthier planet.

              Data Source

              Uses Sentinel-2 MSI L2A imagery (10 m resolution) from ESA/Copernicus, processed via Google Earth Engine. Global coverage, 5-day revisit cycle.

              Spectral Indices

              • NDWI — Normalised Difference Water Index: water presence and clarity.
              • NDTI — Normalised Difference Turbidity Index: suspended sediment and turbidity.
              • FAI — Floating Algae Index: algal blooms and surface scum.

              Scoring Model

              Weighted formula combining all three indices:

              Score = 0.45 × NDTI + 0.35 × FAI + 0.20 × (1 − NDWI)

              All indices normalised to [0,1] before weighting.

              Classification

              ScoreStatusMeaning
              0–30SafeClean water
              30–60ModerateElevated concern
              60–100PollutedHigh pollution risk

              Confidence Levels

              • High — ≥5 images, <10% cloud cover, clear signal
              • Medium — 2–4 images or moderate cloud cover
              • Low — <2 images or high cloud cover, noisy data

              Disclaimer

              AquaVision provides indicative water quality assessments based on optical remote sensing indices. It cannot detect heavy metals, bacteria, or exact chemical pollutants. Results should be validated with in-situ measurements before making public health or regulatory decisions. Cloud cover, atmospheric conditions, and seasonal variation can affect accuracy.
